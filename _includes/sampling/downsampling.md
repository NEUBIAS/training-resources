<h4 id='downsampling'><a href="#downsampling">Downsample an image</a></h4>

A good way to explore what one can still see at different spatial samplings is to acquire an image with very fine sampling and then downsample it in a software. This can inform you how you would then acquire more images with optimal sampling of the microscope. 

The two example images show the Z-maximal projection of several cells at a pixel-size of 35 nm (Zeiss Airy scan).

- Open the image [xy_16bit__DNA_sampling_35nm.tif](https://github.com/NEUBIAS/training-resources/raw/master/image_data/xy_16bit__DNA_sampling_35nm.tif). 
- Perform a sequence of down-sampling operations and observe which biological structures you can still observe at the various sampling levels
- Repeat with image [xy_16bit__MT_sampling_35nm.tif](https://github.com/NEUBIAS/training-resources/raw/master/image_data/xy_16bit__MT_sampling_35nm.tif)
